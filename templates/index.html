About the Belly Button Biodiversity Project

The belly button is teeming with microorganisms! In 2011â€“2012, North Carolina State University's Rob Dunn Lab decided to unravel the microbiome mysteries of the navel. It swabbed the belly buttons of hundreds of volunteers. The swabs were plated and the microorganisms were allowed to grow, providing a "portrait" of the microorganisms that live there. Then, the lab took a step further: scientists used DNA fingerprinting technology to identify the microorganisms at the species level. Over 3600 species (OTUs) were reported in just 153 volunteers!
