About the Belly Button Biodiversity Project

The belly button is teeming with microorganisms! In 2011â€“2012, North Carolina State University's Rob Dunn Lab decided to unravel the microbiome mysteries of the navel. It swabbed the belly buttons of hundreds of volunteers. The swabs were plated

and the microorganisms were allowed to grow, providing a "portrait" of the microorganisms that live there. Then, the lab took a step further: scientists used DNA fingerprinting technology to identify the microorganisms at the species level, based on the gene that

expresses the 16S RNA. Over 3600 species (OTUs) were reported in just 153 volunteers! Explore the dashboard and see the types of microorganisms that were found. Then choose a participant to figure out which microbes teeming in their belly buttons.
